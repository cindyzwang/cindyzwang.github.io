Intro

When I got to college, I had no idea what I wanted to do. I had some AP credits that gave me the freedom to take whatever I wanted for a bit and I took some really interesting classes: linguistics, globalization and poverty, fencing...

But I knew I wanted to do something in the STEM field so I googled Boston University. The Wikipedia page said it had the 8th best Biomedical Engineering program in the country. So I googled "biomedical engineering" and it sounded great. I took plenty of biology-based courses and liked them but my favorite things to do in practice were my technology projects, both software and electronics. And it turns out, working with data is both enjoyable and intuitive to me. So after college, I went to App Academy to learn full-stack web development and now, I'm looking to build software tools.

About

You can download my resume  here.

I grew up in St. Louis, went to Boston University for a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering, and moved out to San Francisco to study software engineering at App Academy. I want to build software tools.

During undergrad, I spent one summer as a research assistant for a neuroscience lab. My job was to write scripts to analyze electroencephalography data. Even though it had been years since I took Neuroscience 101, the data was completely clear to me. After doing the analyses, I added a user interface that let other researchers run and customize the anaylses themselves. This project was not only easy, but fun for me. Creating tools like this is something that I'm passionate about because I get to solve technical problems that help others advance towards their goals.
